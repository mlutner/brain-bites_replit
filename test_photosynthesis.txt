Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize foods from carbon dioxide and water. Photosynthesis in plants generally involves the green pigment chlorophyll and generates oxygen as a byproduct. The overall equation for photosynthesis is: 6CO2 + 6H2O + light energy = C6H12O6 + 6O2. This process occurs in two main stages: the light-dependent reactions (occurring in the thylakoids) and the light-independent reactions or Calvin cycle (occurring in the stroma of chloroplasts).
